Internship

For my first semester in this academic year, I spent it doing a work placement at the company SPI (Styrene Packaging & Insulation Ltd), which is the UK largest producer of styrene. For this job, I worked in the office providing IT support and working on the website. Some of the duties that were included for IT support; setting up email accounts for staff, setting their computers and wiring devices to the network. This was a nine to four job require me to work thirty-five hours a week.


SPI manufacture and supply a range of EPS insulation products, including Stylize EPS insulation and Stylite Plustherm Enhanced EPS insulation. Our building insulation products are suited to building interiors, exteriors, floors, roofs, civil engineering applications and many more. Our polystyrene packaging division also manufactures and supplies a variety of industries with solutions to their packaging needs.



A work placement was a great experience and something I felt I needed to do. Improving my skills and allowing me to gain a better understanding of the working world. I had a list of personal aim and objectives I wanted to receive, such as improve upon my skill in me choose the subject of IT and gaining personal skills.
